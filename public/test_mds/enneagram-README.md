# Enneagram Test - Technical Documentation

## 1. Test Overview

**Test Name:** Enneagram Personality Test  
**Test Code:** `enneagram`  
**Test ID:** `test_007`  
**Purpose:** Personality typing based on core motivations, desires, and fears  
**Measures:** 9 personality types based on ancient spiritual traditions  
**Questions:** 105 statements  
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes  
**Response Type:** 5-point Likert scale (Inaccurate → Accurate)  
**Weight in Analysis:** 20%

---

## 2. Test Description

The Enneagram is a personality typing system based on ancient spiritual traditions which describes people in terms of their core motivations, desires and fears.

The word "enneagram" comes from the Greek "ennea," meaning nine, and "gramma," meaning something drawn or written. The Enneagram describes nine personality types and maps each of these types on a nine-pointed diagram which helps to illustrate how the types relate to one another.

**Key Features:**
- Used widely in counseling, business and spiritual settings
- Helps people better understand what drives them
- Illuminates how you view the world
- Helps discover the roots of who you are
- Increases empathy and understanding of others
- Reveals distinct paths to personal happiness and growth

**What Makes It Different:**
Unlike tests that measure behaviors or preferences, the Enneagram focuses on:
- **Core motivations** - what drives you at your deepest level
- **Core fears** - what you fundamentally want to avoid
- **Core beliefs** - how you fundamentally see the world
- **Worldview** - the lens through which you interpret everything

---

## 3. Test Format

**Structure:**
- 105 self-descriptive statements
- 5-point Likert scale for each statement
- No time limit (typically takes 10-15 minutes)
- Statements distributed across 9 types

**Scale:**
1. **Inaccurate** - This statement does not describe me at all
2. **Mostly Inaccurate** - This statement rarely describes me
3. **Neutral** - Unsure or sometimes true, sometimes not
4. **Mostly Accurate** - This statement often describes me
5. **Accurate** - This statement describes me very well

**Question Distribution:**
- Each of the 9 types has approximately 11-12 questions
- Questions are intermixed (not grouped by type)
- Some questions are worded positively, others negatively
- Includes reverse-scored items

## 4. The Nine Enneagram Types

### Type 1: The Perfectionist
**Core Motivation:** To be good, to have integrity, to be balanced  
**Core Fear:** Being corrupt, evil, or defective  
**Core Desire:** To be right, to be good

**Key Characteristics:**
- Place emphasis on following rules and doing things correctly
- Extremely strict with themselves and others
- Strive for perfection in all areas
- Have a strong inner critic
- Focus on self-improvement

**At Their Best:**
- Wise, discerning, realistic, and noble
- Morally heroic and inspiring

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become critical, perfectionistic, and judgmental
- May develop obsessive-compulsive tendencies

**Sample Questions:**
- "I strive for perfection"
- "I check carefully for mistakes and errors"
- "I hold myself to very high standards"

---

### Type 2: The Giver
**Core Motivation:** To be loved, to be needed  
**Core Fear:** Being unwanted or unworthy of being loved  
**Core Desire:** To feel loved

**Key Characteristics:**
- Want to be liked and find ways to be helpful
- Focus on meeting others' needs
- Warm, caring, and empathetic
- May neglect their own needs
- Seek to be indispensable

**At Their Best:**
- Unselfish, altruistic, and have unconditional love for others
- Give without expecting anything in return

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become people-pleasing and possessive
- May feel entitled to appreciation

**Sample Questions:**
- "I work hard to be helpful to others"
- "I like to be around people who I can help"
- "I am a natural caretaker"

---

### Type 3: The Achiever
**Core Motivation:** To be valuable, to be admired  
**Core Fear:** Being worthless or without value  
**Core Desire:** To feel valuable and worthwhile

**Key Characteristics:**
- Want to be successful and admired
- Very conscious of their public image
- Goal-oriented and ambitious
- Adapt to meet expectations
- Focus on achievement and productivity

**At Their Best:**
- Self-accepting, authentic, and inspiring role models
- Genuinely believe in themselves

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become obsessed with image and status
- May be dishonest or opportunistic

**Sample Questions:**
- "It is important to me to achieve great things"
- "I want to be a person that others can look up to"
- "I set ambitious goals for myself"

---

### Type 4: The Individualist
**Core Motivation:** To be themselves, to find themselves  
**Core Fear:** Having no identity or personal significance  
**Core Desire:** To be unique and authentic

**Key Characteristics:**
- Want to be unique and experience deep emotions
- Feel they are fundamentally different from others
- Overly focused on how they differ from others
- Creative and sensitive
- May feel misunderstood

**At Their Best:**
- Inspired, highly creative, and able to renew themselves
- Self-aware and introspective

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become self-absorbed and temperamental
- May feel they are uniquely flawed

**Sample Questions:**
- "I feel my emotions very deeply"
- "I have a sense that other people will never truly understand me"
- "I don't fit in with ordinary people"

---

### Type 5: The Investigator
**Core Motivation:** To be capable and competent  
**Core Fear:** Being useless, helpless, or incompetent  
**Core Desire:** To be capable and competent

**Key Characteristics:**
- Seek understanding and knowledge
- More comfortable with data than people
- Need privacy and independence
- Minimize their needs
- Detached and analytical

**At Their Best:**
- Visionary pioneers, often ahead of their time
- Can comprehend the world in new ways

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become isolated and eccentric
- May detach from reality

**Sample Questions:**
- "I think deeply about things"
- "I take time to understand things more deeply than most people do"
- "I want to learn as much as I can about how the world works"

---

### Type 6: The Skeptic
**Core Motivation:** To have security and support  
**Core Fear:** Being without support or guidance  
**Core Desire:** To have security and feel supported

**Key Characteristics:**
- Preoccupied with security and safety
- Like to be prepared for problems
- Vigilant and responsible
- May be anxious or suspicious
- Seek authority and guidance

**At Their Best:**
- Internally stable, self-reliant, and courageous
- Trust themselves and others

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become defensive, evasive, and anxious
- May develop paranoid thinking

**Sample Questions:**
- "I am prepared for any disaster"
- "I am concerned about security more than anything else"
- "I always have a plan for what I would do if things go wrong"

---

### Type 7: The Enthusiast
**Core Motivation:** To be satisfied and content  
**Core Fear:** Being deprived and in pain  
**Core Desire:** To be satisfied and happy

**Key Characteristics:**
- Want to have as much fun and adventure as possible
- Easily bored and seek stimulation
- Fear experiencing emotional pain, especially sadness
- Actively avoid pain by staying busy
- Spontaneous and optimistic

**At Their Best:**
- Highly responsive, excitable, and grateful for life
- Joyous and appreciative

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become scattered and undisciplined
- May be escapist and impulsive

**Sample Questions:**

- "I am always up for a new adventure"

### Type 8: The Challenger
**Core Motivation:** To protect themselves  
**Core Fear:** Being harmed or controlled by others  
**Core Desire:** To protect themselves and control their own life

**Key Characteristics:**

- Seek to stand up for what they believe in
- Fear being powerless
- Focus on controlling their environment
- Direct and assertive

**At Their Best:**
- Self-mastering, use their strength to improve others' lives

**Key Characteristics:**
- Like to go with the flow
- Let others set the agenda

- Tend to be passive and agreeable
- Seek harmony and avoid conflict

**At Their Best:**
- Indomitable and all-embracing
- Able to bring people together

**At Their Worst:**
- Can become complacent and stubborn
- May tune out reality

**Sample Questions:**

## 5. The Enneagram System

### Triads (Centers of Intelligence)

The nine types are divided into three centers of intelligence:

#### Heart Types (Types 2, 3, 4)
**Center:** Emotional Intelligence  
**Focus:** Image, identity, and being valued by others  
**Core Emotion:** Shame

**Characteristics:**
- Depend on emotional intelligence to understand reactions
- Concerned with how they are perceived
- Navigate the world through relationships
- Issue: Managing their self-image

**Types:**
- **Type 2:** Focuses on being loved and needed
- **Type 3:** Focuses on being successful and admired
- **Type 4:** Focuses on being unique and authentic

---

#### Head Types (Types 5, 6, 7)
**Center:** Intellectual Intelligence  
**Focus:** Security, safety, and certainty  
**Core Emotion:** Fear

**Characteristics:**
- Depend on intellectual intelligence to make sense of things
- Concerned with thinking and planning
- Navigate the world through analysis
- Issue: Managing anxiety and fear

**Types:**
- **Type 5:** Manages fear by withdrawing and gaining knowledge
- **Type 6:** Manages fear by seeking security and guidance
- **Type 7:** Manages fear by seeking positive experiences

---

#### Body Types (Types 8, 9, 1)
**Center:** Instinctual Intelligence  
**Focus:** Autonomy, control, and boundaries  
**Core Emotion:** Anger

**Characteristics:**
- Depend on instinctual intelligence to follow their "gut"
- Concerned with control and resistance
- Navigate the world through action
- Issue: Managing anger and resistance

**Types:**
- **Type 8:** Expresses anger directly and assertively
- **Type 9:** Represses anger to maintain peace
- **Type 1:** Channels anger into perfectionism

---

### Wings

**Definition:** Wings are the two Enneagram types found on either side of your core type.

**How Wings Work:**
- Each type has two wings (the adjacent numbers on the Enneagram)
- Example: Type 9 has wings of 8 and 1
- Example: Type 4 has wings of 3 and 5
- You can identify with one wing strongly (e.g., "I'm a 4w3")
- Or you can access both wings as you develop

**Wing Possibilities:**
```
Type 1: Wings 9 and 2
Type 2: Wings 1 and 3
Type 3: Wings 2 and 4
Type 4: Wings 3 and 5
Type 5: Wings 4 and 6
Type 6: Wings 5 and 7
Type 7: Wings 6 and 8
Type 8: Wings 7 and 9
Type 9: Wings 8 and 1
```

**Purpose of Wings:**
- Offer new but accessible ways of approaching thought and behavior
- Provide flexibility for growth
- Add nuance to your primary type
- Allow you to develop additional facets of yourself

---

### Arrows (Lines of Integration and Disintegration)

**Definition:** Each type is connected by two lines to two other types, showing paths of growth and stress.

#### Forward Arrow (Line of Integration)
**Direction:** Points away from core type  
**When:** Under healthy development  
**Result:** Type grows into higher state of development

**Example:**
- Type 1 → Type 7 (becomes more spontaneous, joyful)
- Type 2 → Type 4 (becomes more authentic, self-aware)
- Type 3 → Type 6 (becomes more loyal, grounded)

#### Against Arrow (Line of Disintegration)
**Direction:** Points toward core type  
**When:** Under stress or unhealthy  
**Result:** Type exhibits characteristics of this type

**Example:**
- Type 1 ← Type 4 (becomes moody, self-absorbed)
- Type 2 ← Type 8 (becomes aggressive, demanding)
- Type 3 ← Type 9 (becomes complacent, unmotivated)

**Complete Arrow Map:**
```
Type 1 → 7 (integration) | 1 ← 4 (disintegration)
Type 2 → 4 (integration) | 2 ← 8 (disintegration)
Type 3 → 6 (integration) | 3 ← 9 (disintegration)
Type 4 → 1 (integration) | 4 ← 2 (disintegration)
Type 5 → 8 (integration) | 5 ← 7 (disintegration)
Type 6 → 9 (integration) | 6 ← 3 (disintegration)
Type 7 → 5 (integration) | 7 ← 1 (disintegration)
Type 8 → 2 (integration) | 8 ← 5 (disintegration)
Type 9 → 3 (integration) | 9 ← 6 (disintegration)
```

## 8. Scoring System

### Raw Score Calculation

Each question is scored on a 1-5 scale. The raw score for each type is the sum of all responses to questions belonging to that type.

```python
def calculate_raw_scores(answers):
    """
    Calculate raw scores for each Enneagram type
    """
    # Initialize scores
    scores = {
        "type_1": 0,
        "type_2": 0,
        "type_3": 0,
        "type_4": 0,
        "type_5": 0,
        "type_6": 0,
        "type_7": 0,
        "type_8": 0,
        "type_9": 0
    }
    
    # Question mapping
    type_mapping = {
        "type_1": [1, 15, 19, 20, 21, 22, 36, 40, 41, 42, 43, 61, 62, 63, 64, 78, 82, 83, 84, 85, 99, 104, 105],
        "type_2": [2, 3, 23, 24, 44, 65, 86],
        "type_3": [4, 5, 25, 26, 45, 46, 47, 66, 67, 68, 87, 88, 89],
        "type_4": [6, 7, 18, 27, 28, 39, 48, 49, 60, 69, 70, 81, 91],
        "type_5": [8, 29, 50, 71, 92],
        "type_6": [9, 16, 17, 30, 37, 38, 51, 57, 58, 59, 72, 79, 80, 93, 100, 101],
        "type_7": [10, 11, 12, 31, 32, 33, 52, 53, 54, 73, 74, 75, 94, 95, 96, 103],
        "type_8": [13, 34, 55, 76, 97],
        "type_9": [14, 35, 56, 77, 98]
    }
    
    # Reverse scored questions
    reverse_scored = [90, 102]
    
    # Calculate scores
    for answer in answers:
        question_id = answer['question_id']
        response = answer['response']  # 1-5
        
        # Handle reverse scoring
        if question_id in reverse_scored:
            response = 6 - response
        
        # Add to appropriate type(s)
        for type_name, questions in type_mapping.items():
            if question_id in questions:
                scores[type_name] += response
    
    return scores
```

### Normalization

Because different types have different numbers of questions, scores need to be normalized:

```python
def normalize_scores(raw_scores):
    """
    Normalize scores to 0-100 scale based on question count
    """
    question_counts = {
        "type_1": 23,
        "type_2": 7,
        "type_3": 13,
        "type_4": 15,
        "type_5": 5,
        "type_6": 16,
        "type_7": 16,
        "type_8": 5,
        "type_9": 5
    }
    
    normalized = {}
    
    for type_name, raw_score in raw_scores.items():
        num_questions = question_counts[type_name]
        min_possible = num_questions * 1
        max_possible = num_questions * 5
        
        # Normalize to 0-100
        normalized[type_name] = (
            (raw_score - min_possible) / (max_possible - min_possible)
        ) * 100
    
    return normalized
```

### Determining Primary Type

```python
def determine_primary_type(normalized_scores):
    """
    Determine the user's primary Enneagram type
    """
    # Find highest score
    primary_type = max(normalized_scores.items(), key=lambda x: x[1])
    
    return {
        "primary_type": primary_type[0],
        "score": primary_type[1]
    }
```

### Determining Wings

```python
def determine_wings(primary_type, normalized_scores):
    """
    Determine wing scores based on adjacent types
    """
    # Wing mapping (adjacent types on Enneagram circle)
    wing_map = {
        "type_1": ["type_9", "type_2"],
        "type_2": ["type_1", "type_3"],
        "type_3": ["type_2", "type_4"],
        "type_4": ["type_3", "type_5"],
        "type_5": ["type_4", "type_6"],
        "type_6": ["type_5", "type_7"],
        "type_7": ["type_6", "type_8"],
        "type_8": ["type_7", "type_9"],
        "type_9": ["type_8", "type_1"]
    }
    
    possible_wings = wing_map[primary_type]
    
    wing_scores = {
        wing: normalized_scores[wing]
        for wing in possible_wings
    }
    
    # Determine dominant wing
    dominant_wing = max(wing_scores.items(), key=lambda x: x[1])
    
    return {
        "wings": possible_wings,
        "wing_scores": wing_scores,
        "dominant_wing": dominant_wing[0],
        "wing_notation": f"{primary_type.split('_')[1]}w{dominant_wing[0].split('_')[1]}"
    }
```

### Determining Triad

```python
def determine_triad(primary_type):
    """
    Determine which center of intelligence (triad) the type belongs to
    """
    triads = {
        "heart": ["type_2", "type_3", "type_4"],
        "head": ["type_5", "type_6", "type_7"],
        "body": ["type_8", "type_9", "type_1"]
    }
    
    for triad_name, types in triads.items():
        if primary_type in types:
            return triad_name
    
    return None
```

## 11. Database Schema

### enneagram_questions

```sql
CREATE TABLE enneagram_questions (
    id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
    question_number INTEGER NOT NULL,
    text TEXT NOT NULL,
    type_scored VARCHAR(10) NOT NULL,  -- 'type_1' through 'type_9'
    reverse_scored BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
    created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Index for fast lookups
CREATE INDEX idx_enneagram_questions_type ON enneagram_questions(type_scored);
```

### enneagram_results

```sql
CREATE TABLE enneagram_results (
    id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
    user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
    test_session_id UUID,
    
    -- Raw scores
    raw_scores JSONB NOT NULL,
    
    -- Normalized scores (0-100)
    normalized_scores JSONB NOT NULL,
    
    -- Primary type
    primary_type VARCHAR(10) NOT NULL,
    primary_type_name VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    primary_type_score DECIMAL(5,2) NOT NULL,
    
    -- Top three types
    top_three_types JSONB NOT NULL,
    
    -- Triad
    triad VARCHAR(10) NOT NULL,  -- 'heart', 'head', or 'body'
    
    -- Wings
    wings JSONB NOT NULL,
    wing_notation VARCHAR(10),  -- e.g., '9w1'
    
    -- Arrows
    integration_arrow VARCHAR(10),
    disintegration_arrow VARCHAR(10),
    
    completed_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
    duration_seconds INTEGER
);

-- Indexes
CREATE INDEX idx_enneagram_results_user ON enneagram_results(user_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_enneagram_results_primary_type ON enneagram_results(primary_type);
CREATE INDEX idx_enneagram_results_triad ON enneagram_results(triad);
```

### enneagram_answers

```sql
CREATE TABLE enneagram_answers (
    id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
    result_id INTEGER REFERENCES enneagram_results(id),
    question_number INTEGER NOT NULL,
    response INTEGER NOT NULL,  -- 1-5
    answered_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Index for fast lookups
CREATE INDEX idx_enneagram_answers_result ON enneagram_answers(result_id);
```

## 15. Type Descriptions for Results

### Type 1: The Perfectionist
**At Their Best:** Wise, discerning, realistic, and noble. Can be morally heroic.  
**At Their Worst:** Critical, perfectionistic, and judgmental. May become obsessive.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to accept imperfection and develop flexibility.

### Type 2: The Giver
**At Their Best:** Unselfish and altruistic, with unconditional love for others.  
**At Their Worst:** People-pleasing, possessive, and manipulative.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to acknowledge and meet your own needs.

### Type 3: The Achiever
**At Their Best:** Self-accepting, authentic, and inspiring role models.  
**At Their Worst:** Obsessed with image, competitive, and potentially dishonest.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to value yourself beyond your achievements.

### Type 4: The Individualist
**At Their Best:** Inspired, creative, and able to renew themselves through self-awareness.  
**At Their Worst:** Self-absorbed, temperamental, and withdrawn.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to appreciate your commonalities with others.

### Type 5: The Investigator
**At Their Best:** Visionary pioneers who comprehend the world in new ways.  
**At Their Worst:** Isolated, eccentric, and detached from reality.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to engage with the world and express your needs.

### Type 6: The Skeptic
**At Their Best:** Internally stable, self-reliant, and courageously supportive.  
**At Their Worst:** Defensive, evasive, anxious, and potentially paranoid.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to trust yourself and develop inner security.

### Type 7: The Enthusiast
**At Their Best:** Highly responsive, excitable, and grateful for life.  
**At Their Worst:** Scattered, undisciplined, escapist, and impulsive.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to sit with difficult emotions and focus deeply.

### Type 8: The Challenger
**At Their Best:** Self-mastering, using strength to improve others' lives.  
**At Their Worst:** Domineering, confrontational, and intimidating.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to be vulnerable and show your softer side.

### Type 9: The Peacemaker
**At Their Best:** Indomitable, all-embracing, able to bring people together.  
**At Their Worst:** Complacent, stubborn, and passive-aggressive.  
**Growth Path:** Learn to assert yourself and prioritize your needs.
