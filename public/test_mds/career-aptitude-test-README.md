## 2. Test Description
What is a Career Test?
A career test measures your unique aptitudes and interests to show you which career paths may be best suited to your individual preferences and traits. A good career test gives you an objective measure of your strengths and talents and maps them to the requirements of real-world jobs.

There are many factors that impact how successful and satisfied you will be in a particular job. Many of these factors—including personality, interests, values, and motivations—can be measured by a career aptitude test. A valid career test will explore as many aspects of your strengths and talents as possible, while providing concrete feedback that you can use to narrow down your job search.

Typically, people take a career test with one of two goals in mind:

To choose a new career path that suits their talents and abilities
To improve their performance on their current career path, through deeper insight into their strengths and weaknesses
A reliable career test will measure key traits that are relevant to your performance and satisfaction in the workplace, and provide you with actionable insights to make important career decisions.

What is the Best Career Test?
There is no single best career test for everyone, as different tests address different goals, life stages, and levels of self awareness. Before choosing a career test, take some time to consider what you hope to learn, so that you can match your needs to the best tool for you. There are various types of career tests you can choose, each with their own benefits. These include:

Personality assessments. This type of test can help you find a career that suits your core personality traits and innate talents. A personality career test will measure your personality traits, and then show you what careers people with similar personality traits have chosen. 
Interest assessments. This type of career test can help you find a job where the day-to-day requirements are a good match for the tasks and activities you enjoy doing. A career interests test will measure how much you like various categories of work tasks, and match you to jobs that are a good fit for those tasks. 
Career aptitude tests. An aptitude test can measure a variety of determinants of job aptitude, including personality and interests, as well as skills like reasoning ability, reading comprehension, or intelligence. Skills-based aptitude tests are more often found when being evaluated for a particular job role, while more general aptitude tests can help you figure out what careers suit your natural inclinations.
To choose the best career test, consider what questions you need to answer to take the next step in your career planning. You may feel you need an objective look at your strengths and weaknesses, or you might be confused about how your skills and abilities can translate into a career path. Different career tests measure different aspects of your career aptitude, so thinking about where your own information gaps lie can help you choose one that will suit your needs.

If you are planning a new career or making a career change, the best career test is one that will give you a comprehensive assessment of your personality, interests, aptitude, and strengths. A multi-dimensional career assessment will help you to consider all the important factors that drive career satisfaction, and plan your career path in a holistic way. Truity's Career Aptitude Test evaluates both your personality and interests, so that you can choose a career that suits your natural talents as well as your real-world experiences.

To ensure you receive an accurate career assessment, be sure to select a test that has been scientifically validated and developed by experts in career planning. Although it is easy to find free career tests online, only some of them are accurate enough to give you reliable career direction. Before taking a career test, check for information about reliability and validity, as well as the reputation of the company that publishes the test.

How Are Career Tests Used?
One of the most effective ways to start figuring out the right career for you is by using a career assessment. These tools help you clarify your strengths, interests, and work style—key ingredients for making a smart, personalized career choice. Validated online career tests make this process easy and accessible. Here’s how the process typically works:

Take an assessment: You’ll answer a series of questions that assess things like your personality traits, values, and preferences.
Review your results: Once you complete the test, you’ll receive a report that outlines key career factors such as your strengths, typical work style, and compatible career paths.
Reflect on the insights: Use your career report to think critically about what matters to you in a career. Do you want variety or stability? Independence or collaboration? A fast-paced environment or something slower and more methodical? The assessment helps you name these preferences so you can prioritize them in your search.
Narrow your options: Instead of trying to explore every possible career, use your results to focus on a smaller group of jobs that align with your natural tendencies. Online resources such as Truity's career search tool help you learn more about each suggested career, making it easier to dig into real-world options that fit you.
Take the next step with confidence: Whether that’s researching educational requirements, networking, or applying for jobs, your assessment results act as a personal guide. They don’t dictate your future—but they do give you a strong starting point and a clearer sense of direction.
In short, a career test can turn a vague question like “What should I do with my life?” into a structured, actionable plan. A good career test can help you move forward with clarity—based on who you are and what’s likely to make you thrive.

Career Test FAQ

Using a unique combination of these two powerful career matching systems, the career test will determine which jobs are most likely to match your aptitude and satisfy your individual personality and motivational factors.

**Question Type:** Activity-based interest assessment

**Focus:** Concrete work activities and tasks

> "To take the career test, mark your interest in each activity shown. Do not worry about whether you have the skills or training to do an activity, or how much money you might make. Simply think about whether you would enjoy doing it or not."

## 8. Scoring System

### Part 1: Interest Scores (3-Point Scale)

**Raw Score Calculation:**

For each of 6 interest areas (R, I, A, S, E, C):

```
Score Range: 9-27 points per area
- Minimum: 9 questions × 1 (all "Dislike") = 9
- Maximum: 9 questions × 3 (all "Like") = 27
```

```python
def calculate_career_aptitude_results(answers_part1, answers_part2):
    """
    Calculate comprehensive career assessment results
    
    Args:
        answers_part1: List of {question_id, rating} for interest questions (1-3)
        answers_part2: List of {question_id, rating} for personality questions (1-5)
    
    Returns:
        Complete result object with interests, personality, and career matches
    """
    
    # STEP 1: Calculate Interest Scores (Part 1)
    interest_scores = calculate_interest_scores(answers_part1)
    
    # STEP 2: Calculate Personality Scores (Part 2)
    personality_scores = calculate_personality_scores(answers_part2)
    
    # STEP 3: Determine Work Style Profile
    work_style = determine_work_style(interest_scores, personality_scores)
    
    # STEP 4: Generate Career Matches
    career_matches = match_careers(interest_scores, personality_scores)
    
    # STEP 5: Create Personalized Recommendations
    recommendations = generate_recommendations(
        interest_scores, 
        personality_scores, 
        work_style
    )
    
    return {
        'interests': interest_scores,
        'personality': personality_scores,
        'work_style': work_style,
        'career_matches': career_matches,
        'recommendations': recommendations
    }

def calculate_interest_scores(answers):
    """Calculate scores for 6 interest areas"""
    
    areas = {'R': [], 'I': [], 'A': [], 'S': [], 'E': [], 'C': []}
    
    # Group answers by category
    for answer in answers:
        question = get_question(answer.question_id)
        areas[question.category].append(answer.rating)
    
    # Calculate raw and normalized scores
    scores = {}
    for area, ratings in areas.items():
        raw_score = sum(ratings)  # Sum of 8 ratings (8-24)
        normalized = ((raw_score - 8) / 16) * 100  # Convert to 0-100
        
        scores[area] = {
            'raw_score': raw_score,
            'normalized_score': round(normalized, 1),
            'level': classify_interest_level(normalized)
        }
    
    # Identify top 3 areas
    sorted_areas = sorted(scores.items(), 
                         key=lambda x: x[1]['normalized_score'], 
                         reverse=True)
    
    scores['top_3'] = [area[0] for area in sorted_areas[:3]]
    scores['primary'] = sorted_areas[0][0]
    
    return scores

def calculate_personality_scores(answers):
    """Calculate scores for 5 personality dimensions"""
    
    dimensions = {'O': [], 'C': [], 'E': [], 'A': [], 'N': []}
    
    # Group and score answers (apply reverse scoring)
    for answer in answers:
        question = get_question(answer.question_id)
        
        if question.reverse_scored:
            score = 6 - answer.rating
        else:
            score = answer.rating
        
        dimensions[question.dimension].append(score)
    
    # Calculate scores
    scores = {}
    for dim, ratings in dimensions.items():
        raw_score = sum(ratings)  # Sum of 4 ratings (4-20)
        normalized = ((raw_score - 4) / 16) * 100  # Convert to 0-100
        
        scores[dim] = {
            'raw_score': raw_score,
            'normalized_score': round(normalized, 1),
            'level': classify_personality_level(dim, normalized)
        }
    
    return scores

def classify_interest_level(score):
    """Classify interest level based on normalized score"""
    if score >= 75:
        return 'Very High'
    elif score >= 60:
        return 'High'
    elif score >= 40:
        return 'Moderate'
    elif score >= 25:
        return 'Low'
    else:
        return 'Very Low'

def classify_personality_level(dimension, score):
    """Classify personality trait level"""
    if score >= 70:
        return 'High'
    elif score >= 55:
        return 'Above Average'
    elif score >= 45:
        return 'Average'
    elif score >= 30:
        return 'Below Average'
    else:
        return 'Low'

def determine_work_style(interests, personality):
    """
    Determine work style profile based on combined scores
    
    Work Style Quadrants:
    - Humanitarian (High S + High O/A)
    - Innovator (High I + High O/C)
    - Pragmatist (High R/C + High C)
    - Caretaker (High S + High C/A)
    """
    
    # Get top interest
    primary_interest = interests['primary']
    
    # Get personality profile
    high_o = personality['O']['normalized_score'] > 60
    high_c = personality['C']['normalized_score'] > 60
    high_a = personality['A']['normalized_score'] > 60
    
    # Determine quadrant
    if primary_interest == 'S' and (high_o or high_a):
        return 'Humanitarian'
    elif primary_interest == 'I' and (high_o or high_c):
        return 'Innovator'
    elif primary_interest in ['R', 'C'] and high_c:
        return 'Pragmatist'
    elif primary_interest == 'S' and (high_c or high_a):
        return 'Caretaker'
    else:
        return determine_custom_profile(interests, personality)

def match_careers(interests, personality):
    """
    Match user profile to career database
    
    Matching Algorithm:
    1. Filter careers by top 3 interest areas
    2. Score each career based on personality fit
    3. Rank by combined score (60% interests + 40% personality)
    4. Return top 20 matches with explanations
    """
    
    top_interests = interests['top_3']
    
    # Get careers matching interest profile
    candidate_careers = get_careers_by_interests(top_interests)
    
    # Score each career
    scored_careers = []
    for career in candidate_careers:
        interest_match = calculate_interest_match(
            interests, 
            career.required_interests
        )
        
        personality_match = calculate_personality_match(
            personality,
            career.ideal_personality
        )
        
        # Combined score (60/40 split)
        total_score = (interest_match * 0.6) + (personality_match * 0.4)
        
        scored_careers.append({
            'career': career,
            'match_score': round(total_score, 1),
            'interest_match': interest_match,
            'personality_match': personality_match,
            'explanation': generate_match_explanation(
                career, 
                interests, 
                personality
            )
        })
    
    # Sort by match score and return top 20
    scored_careers.sort(key=lambda x: x['match_score'], reverse=True)
    return scored_careers[:20]

def calculate_interest_match(user_interests, career_interests):
    """
    Calculate interest match score (0-100)
    
    Compares user's interest profile with career's required interests
    """
    match_score = 0
    
    for interest_area, required_level in career_interests.items():
        user_level = user_interests[interest_area]['normalized_score']
        
        # Calculate distance from requirement
        if user_level >= required_level:
            match_score += 100  # Perfect match or better
        else:
            # Partial credit based on how close
            match_score += (user_level / required_level) * 100
    
    # Average across all interest areas
    return match_score / len(career_interests)

def calculate_personality_match(user_personality, ideal_personality):
    """
    Calculate personality fit score (0-100)
    
    Compares user personality with ideal traits for career
    """
    match_scores = []
    
    for trait, ideal_range in ideal_personality.items():
        user_score = user_personality[trait]['normalized_score']
        
        # Check if user score falls within ideal range
        if ideal_range['min'] <= user_score <= ideal_range['max']:
            match_scores.append(100)
        else:
            # Calculate distance from range
            if user_score < ideal_range['min']:
                distance = ideal_range['min'] - user_score
            else:
                distance = user_score - ideal_range['max']
            
            # Penalize based on distance
            penalty = min(distance * 2, 100)  # Max penalty 100
            match_scores.append(max(0, 100 - penalty))
    
    # Average personality match
    return sum(match_scores) / len(match_scores)
```

```sql
        (part = 1 AND rating BETWEEN 1 AND 3) OR part != 1
    ),
    CONSTRAINT check_rating_part2 CHECK (
        (part = 2 AND rating BETWEEN 1 AND 5) OR part != 2
    )
);

CREATE INDEX idx_career_answers_result ON career_answers(result_id);
```

### career_matches

```sql
CREATE TABLE career_matches (
    id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
    result_id INTEGER REFERENCES career_test_results(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
    
    profession_id INTEGER REFERENCES professions(id),
    match_score DECIMAL(5,2),
    interest_match DECIMAL(5,2),
    personality_match DECIMAL(5,2),
    rank INTEGER,
    
    created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

CREATE INDEX idx_career_matches_result ON career_matches(result_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_career_matches_rank ON career_matches(result_id, rank);
```

## Scientific Background

**Holland Code (RIASEC):**
- Developed by John L. Holland (1959)
- Based on 50+ years of research
- Most widely used career assessment
- Validated across cultures
- Foundation of O*NET career database

**Big Five Personality:**
- Consensus model in personality psychology
- Decades of validation research
- Predicts job performance and satisfaction
- Cross-culturally reliable
- Used in organizational psychology

**Combined Assessment:**
- Interests predict satisfaction
- Personality predicts performance
- Together: comprehensive career fit
- 60/40 weighting based on research
- Validated approach in career counseling
